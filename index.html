I'm Luke, a Research Assistant at the Federal Reserve Board.

I studied economics at the University of Washington, where my honors thesis evaluated a controvesial criminal justice reform policy's impact on California's retail sector. Alongside my studies, I served a stint as a research assistant at the Evans School of Public Policy and worked as an analyst at a Pacific Northwest-focused venture capital firm.

At the Fed, my research largely relates to finance and industrial organization; my policy work concerns the financial stability and competitive impacts of bank mergers.

Outside of work I enjoy playing basketball, logging new films on Letterboxd, and amateur-grade photography of my adventures.
